# The Skitch Family Archive

A living collection of family history — stories, records, and memories gathered from letters, photographs, and the recollections of those who lived them.

### 📖 Read the archive → **<https://chris-skitch.github.io/family-history/>**

---

The Skitch name arrived in Western Australia aboard the lives of Cornish emigrants. What followed was five generations of foresters and farmers, singers and servicemen — ordinary people living through extraordinary times. These documents are an attempt to preserve what might otherwise be forgotten.

The collection begins with Bob Skitch — known to his family as Junior — and his account of growing up in the 1930s and 40s in a Western Australia still shaped by the rhythms of the bush. More volumes will follow as they are transcribed and prepared.

## How this archive is made

Each memoir is published in two synchronised forms:

- A **web edition** here, with the original photographs and house-style typography.
- A **NotebookLM-safe edition** — a Google Doc with the photographs rendered as silhouettes, so the stories can be explored conversationally in [NotebookLM](https://notebooklm.google.com/). (NotebookLM filters photographs out of uploaded sources; the silhouettes come through, and each caption links back to the original here.)

Both are produced from a single source document by **[MemoirForge](https://github.com/chris-skitch/MemoirForge)** — an open-source pipeline for turning family memoirs into synchronised web + NotebookLM editions.
